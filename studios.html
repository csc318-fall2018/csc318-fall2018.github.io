Each tutorial will be used as a critique session (i.e., studio), where every group hast to present their weekly progress to the rest of the class. Each group has 7 minutes to present the deliverables listed for the studios listed below. After each presentation is a 10-12 minute discussion period, during which time students from other groups are expected to critique the presented work. Everyone is expected to participate in the studio sessions; your engagement in each studio class will be graded based on your active participation to class, e.g. by asking questions, answering questions, making comments after presentations, etc... Additionally, every student must submit write-ups of their critiques of the other groups that presented in their class by 12:00pm (noon) sharp on the day after each studio.

Deliverables

Oct 4: Formative study design, instruments, and protocols

  • Problem statement
  • Design of formative study #1 (instruments, protocol)
  • Draft of formative study #2 (what method, initial ideas)

Oct 11: Data collection & analysis planning

  • Results of study 1
  • Refined problem statement
  • Design of formative study #2 (instruments and methods)

Oct 18: Analysis results, design requirements, and job stories

  • Results of formative study
  • Design requirements/job stories
  • Experience maps

Oct 25: High level design concepts & design walkthrough

  • Sketches of many different design concepts
  • Best three design concepts & assessment of the pros and cons of each
  • Sketches of a refined design concept
  • Storyboards explaining how the refined design concept works

Nov 1: Expert evaluation of low fidelity prototype (in class)

  • Paper prototype
  • Discount usability study (either heuristic evaluation or cognitive walkthrough; this will be conducted in class
  • Additional discount usability study results (think aloud, heuristic evaluation, or cognitive walkthrough; to be done outside of class separately afterwards)

Nov 15: High fidelity prototype, summative study design, instruments, and protocols

  • Findings from discount usability studies with low fidelity prototype
  • High fidelity prototype
  • Instruments for experiment

Nov 22: Study results, expected revisions to the design

  • Results
  • Conclusions about the design
  • Future work
  • Demo of your prototype with instructors (to be scheduled and done outside of class)

Nov 29: Project presentation (within tutorial competition)

  • Project presentation

Dec 6: Competition finals
